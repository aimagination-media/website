# AImagination Studio — Website Design Brief

> **Audience:** External design team
> **Purpose:** Fresh design perspective on the AImagination Studio portfolio website
> **Tone:** We want honest creative input — you are not constrained by what exists today

---

## What Is AImagination Studio?

AImagination Studio is an AI-powered educational content production engine. We use a custom pipeline (Markdown → Manim animations → FFmpeg → TTS) to produce polished educational videos at scale across multiple subjects and languages.

Our content lives on YouTube across several channels. The website is the studio's public face: a portfolio hub where visitors can discover our channels, browse videos, and connect with the community.

**We produce content in three main areas:**
- **Mathematics** — animated explanations from basics to advanced topics
- **Chemistry** — visual walkthroughs of reactions and concepts
- **Audiobooks** — narrated educational literature

**We publish in three languages:** English, Spanish, and German — each with its own set of channels and content calendar.

---

## Who Is This Website For?

**Primary visitor:** A curious learner (15–35 years old) who found one of our YouTube videos and wants to explore more. They care about quality and depth, not gimmicks. They may not speak English as a first language.

**Secondary visitor:** A content professional or educator evaluating us as a resource — they want to quickly understand our scope and credibility.

**What they need from the site:**
1. Instantly understand what kind of content we make
2. Browse and discover videos across channels and subjects
3. Find content in their preferred language
4. Follow us on social platforms or subscribe

---

## Pages and Structure

The website has two main layers:

### 1. Main Portfolio Hub (`index.html`)

The core experience. A single page that aggregates content from all channels and languages.

**What it must do:**
- Show the brand identity prominently
- Let visitors filter content by language (EN / ES / DE / All)
- Let visitors filter by content type (long-form videos vs. shorts)
- Let visitors filter by channel (Math, Chemistry, Audiobooks, AI Gallery, etc.)
- Show featured playlists (curated series) in a dedicated section
- Show latest uploads in a browsable grid
- Show upcoming/scheduled videos (content coming soon)
- Surface social links (YouTube channels, Instagram, TikTok, Patreon)
- Search across all videos by title, channel, or subject
- Work well on mobile — this is a significant use case

**Three content views the visitor can toggle between:**
- **Videos** — the main browsable feed of individual videos
- **Playlists** — curated series/collections
- **Socials** — cards linking to our social profiles

### 2. Channel Pages (`/en/channel.html`, `/es/channel.html`, `/de/channel.html`)

Language-specific landing pages that show our channels for a given language. Less visited than the hub, but important for language-first SEO and direct navigation.

## Functional Requirements (Non-Negotiable)

These are constraints the design must accommodate — they come from real user needs and technical realities.

| Requirement | Why |
|---|---|
| Multilingual UI (EN / ES / DE) | Significant share of traffic is non-English |
| Language picker visible at all times | Users switch languages mid-session |
| Video thumbnail grid | Content is visual; thumbnails drive clicks |
| Channel filter chips | We have 10+ channels; users need to narrow down |
| Fuse.js powered search | In-page search with no backend round-trip |
| Scheduled video visibility | "Coming soon" cards build anticipation |
| Social media section | Core growth channel; must be easy to reach |
| Mobile bottom navigation | ~50% of traffic is mobile |
| Fast load without JS | Content still renders with progressive enhancement |
| GitHub Pages hosting | Static files only — no SSR, no server-side logic |

## Data Model (What We're Displaying)

Everything comes from a JSON file generated by our backend. Each item has:

**Video card:**
- Title
- Thumbnail (YouTube image)
- Published date (or "scheduled" state with future date)
- Channel name + channel accent color
- Language
- Series/playlist association
- Video type (long-form 4K or short)

**Playlist card:**
- Title
- Thumbnail (from first video)
- Channel name
- Video count

**Social card:**
- Platform (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Patreon)
- Channel name + handle
- Subscriber/follower count (where available)

## Current Pain Points (Honest Assessment)

We want the design team to help us solve these:

1. **The language selector feels buried.** Visitors who land on English content and speak Spanish often miss that a Spanish version exists.

2. **The filter system is functional but cluttered.** Two rows of filters (video type + channel) stack up and eat vertical space, especially on mobile.

3. **Playlists are underserved.** A curated series is a strong discovery hook, but right now playlists are a tab hidden behind the main feed. Many visitors never find them.

4. **The "upcoming videos" experience is weak.** Scheduled content is shown inline with published content, just with a visual badge. It could be a stronger "what's coming" moment.

5. **Social proof is an afterthought.** We have tens of thousands of subscribers across channels. This isn't communicated.

6. **The brand feels like a generic dark-mode SaaS.** We make creative educational content — the site should feel more alive, curious, and distinct.

## What We're Open to Redesigning

Everything visual is on the table:
- Color palette and theming
- Typography system
- Layout paradigm (bento grid, scroll storytelling, sidebar, masonry — anything)
- Navigation pattern
- Iconography style
- Animation/motion approach
- Information architecture of the sections

What is **not** open to change (technical constraints):
- Static HTML/CSS/JS only (GitHub Pages)
- Data comes from a JSON file at a URL
- Three-language support is a hard requirement
- YouTube thumbnail embeds will remain the primary visual element

---

## Brand Personality

We want the site to feel:

- **Curious and rigorous** — not dumbed down, built for learners who take education seriously
- **Multilingual and globally minded** — not just "translated English", but genuinely international
- **AI-native but human** — we use AI tools heavily, but the content is crafted with care
- **Modern without being cold** — contrast with academic stuffiness, but don't go full startup-bro

We currently use the name **"AImagination Studio"** and the abbreviated branding **"AImaginationX"** across social. The "X" is a nod to multiplied possibilities (cross-language, cross-subject, cross-format) — not affiliated with X/Twitter.

---

## Deliverables We're Asking For

We're not prescribing the format. We'd love to see:

1. **Mood board or visual direction** — what personality/aesthetic direction feels right
2. **Key screen mockups** — at minimum the main portfolio hub desktop and mobile
3. **Component thinking** — how you'd approach the video card, filter system, and language picker
4. **Notes on what you'd challenge** — if any of our stated requirements feel wrong, tell us

We are genuinely open to hearing "this page is trying to do too much" or "the language model here is backwards." Design critique is as valuable as design proposals.
